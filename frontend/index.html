? How Sahl Works

Six steps from raw fight data to honest probabilities you can reason about.

STEP 01

Learn from history

XGBoost — gradient-boosted decision trees — trains on thousands of past UFC fights. It learns patterns from 24 features per matchup including Elo skill rating, striking volume, takedown defense, age, and reach.

STEP 02

Calibrate the probabilities

Raw model outputs aren't true probabilities. Isotonic regression on a held-out validation set maps them so 70% really means 70%. Calibration matters more than accuracy for honest analysis.

STEP 03

Find market disagreements

Sahl strips the vig out of your sportsbook's odds to find their implied probability, then compares to its own. When they meaningfully disagree, that's a spot worth a closer look.

STEP 04

Quantify the edge

Half-Kelly stake math with a 5% bankroll cap shows you what a disciplined bettor would risk. EV greater than 2% triggers a "value" tag. Below that, the edge is too thin to chase.

STEP 05

Explain every pick

Real SHAP values show the top 5 reasons the model picked who it picked. Elo difference too big? Takedown rate mismatch? You see exactly what drove the prediction — no black box.

STEP 06

Self-learn weekly

Every Monday, Sahl scrapes the previous week's UFC results, updates Elo ratings, and retrains itself. Next week's reads are sharper than this week's.

REALITY CHECK State-of-the-art UFC prediction tops out at ~70% accuracy. MMA is genuinely high-variance — injuries, weight cuts, judging chaos. Sahl's value isn't picking every winner. It's giving you calibrated probabilities and a transparent breakdown of why. The decision to bet, pass, or watch is yours.
